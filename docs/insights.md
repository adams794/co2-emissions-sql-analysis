# 🌍 Global CO₂ Emissions by Continent

Carbon dioxide (CO₂) emissions have increased significantly since the Industrial Revolution, driven by industrialization, economic growth, and rising energy demand. While early emissions were concentrated in a single region, the global distribution has shifted substantially over time.

## 📈 Key Insights

- **Early emissions were highly concentrated in Europe**  
  In the 18th and early 19th century, nearly all recorded CO₂ emissions originated from Europe, reflecting its central role in early industrial development.

- **The center of emissions shifted in the 20th century**  
  North America became the dominant contributor during the first half of the 20th century, driven by rapid industrial expansion and economic growth.

- **Asia is now the primary source of global emissions**  
  From the late 20th century onward, emissions in Asia increased rapidly, eventually surpassing all other regions combined.

- **Post-1950 marks a clear acceleration in global emissions**  
  The period commonly referred to as the “Great Acceleration” corresponds with a sharp increase in emissions across multiple regions.

- **Other regions contribute relatively smaller shares**  
  Africa, South America, and Oceania show gradual growth over time but remain significantly lower in absolute terms compared to major industrial regions.
